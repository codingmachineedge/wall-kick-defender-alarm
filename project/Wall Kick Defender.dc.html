An Aqara sensor mounted on your side of the shared wall reads every thump, kick and thunderous footstep transmitted through the drywall. Golden rule: never, ever trust the client.

07 — INTERROGATION

Reasonable questions.

Isn't this a bit petty?

Yes. Pettiness is the core feature. He kicked first — we simply built infrastructure around it.

Will it wake ME up too?

The speaker lives in your room but faces the shared wall point-blank, firing the ringtone through the drywall into his side. A little bleed-through on your end is a small tax on justice — and honestly, the sound of him waking up is deeply soothing.

Do I need Home Assistant?

Yep — the Defender runs entirely on your local Home Assistant setup with the Aqara sensor. Fully offline, no cloud, no subscription, no mercy.

What if he removes the sensor?

The Defender detects tampering and files it under the Shame Counter as "Attempted Cover-Up." Escalation is automatic.

Does the speaker record everything?

No. It only saves a short local receipt after the AI confirms a wall impact. We are here to prove one kick, not become a weird bedroom podcast.

Can he still say "it wasn't me"?

Technically yes. The dashboard will place his denial next to a timestamp, confidence score and waveform, then look disappointed in him.
